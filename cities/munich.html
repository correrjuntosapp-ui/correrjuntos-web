Munich's Green Running Paradise

Munich offers runners exceptional green spaces and river trails within an elegant Bavarian city. The massive Englischer Garten rivals Central Park in size, whilst the Isar River Trail provides scenic long-run routes. CorrerJuntos connects you with Munich's organized, outdoor-loving running community that embraces all seasons.

Whether you're training for the Munich Marathon in October, running through Olympiapark's 1972 Olympic facilities, or exploring Nymphenburg Palace's manicured grounds, Munich combines natural beauty with German efficiency. Find your crew and experience Bavarian running culture.

Best Neighborhoods for Running in Munich

Each Munich neighborhood offers its own character and running experience. These are the top areas for runners in the Bavarian capital:

Schwabing / Englischer Garten

Schwabing is the epicenter of Munich's running scene thanks to its direct access to the Englischer Garten. This massive 375-hectare park stretches north from the city center with over 78 km of paths through open meadows, shaded forest trails, and along the Eisbach and Schwabinger Bach streams. Classic loops of 5, 8, or 12 km are easy to map out, passing landmarks like the Chinese Tower beer garden and the Monopteros hilltop temple. The flat terrain suits all levels, while the park's sheer size means you can run for hours without repeating a path. Most local running clubs and group runs start here.

Isarvorstadt / Glockenbachviertel

Isarvorstadt places runners right along the Isar River, Munich's natural backbone. The restored riverbanks offer gravel trails and paved paths running north-south through the city, perfect for flat, uninterrupted long runs. From the Deutsches Museum island you can head south toward Flaucher, a beloved riverside area with gravel beaches and shaded paths under old trees. Heading north connects you to the Englischer Garten. The adjacent Glockenbachviertel is one of Munich's liveliest neighborhoods, with countless cafes for post-run refueling and a creative, community-minded atmosphere that makes social running groups thrive here.

Olympiapark / Milbertshofen

The 1972 Olympic grounds remain one of Munich's most versatile running venues. The park features a 3 km perimeter loop around the Olympic lake with views of the iconic tent-roof stadium and television tower. For hill training, Olympiaberg provides a steep 60-meter climb that rewards with panoramic views of the city skyline and, on clear days, the Alpine range to the south. The surrounding Milbertshofen area offers flat residential streets for warm-up jogs, and the park connects northward to the BMW World complex and Luitpoldpark for extended route options.

Nymphenburg

The Nymphenburg Palace complex offers one of Munich's most elegant running settings. The formal palace gardens stretch west with wide, tree-lined avenues and geometrically perfect canal paths. The Nymphenburger Kanal itself provides a straight, flat 2 km stretch that is popular for tempo runs and intervals. Beyond the formal gardens, the adjacent Botanischer Garten and the wilder Schlosspark Nymphenburg extend the route options through landscaped parkland. This area is quieter than the Englischer Garten, making it ideal for runners who prefer a more peaceful and scenic environment.

Au-Haidhausen

On the east bank of the Isar, Au-Haidhausen combines riverside running with access to some of Munich's most charming streets. The Maximiliansanlagen park runs along elevated terrain above the river, offering shaded paths with views down to the water. From here, runners can cross to the Isar's west bank trails or head south along the river toward the Tierpark Hellabrunn zoo and the Flaucher gravel beaches. Haidhausen's Wiener Platz and the surrounding cafe culture make it a natural gathering point for running groups. The neighborhood's hillier terrain compared to the flat Isar paths also adds welcome variety for training.

Major Running Events in Munich

Munich's race calendar offers events for every distance and ambition throughout the year:

  • Munich Marathon (October) - The city's flagship race takes runners past Olympiapark, through sections of the Englischer Garten, and along the Isar River. The flat course and autumn temperatures make it a strong choice for personal bests. Over 20,000 runners participate across marathon, half marathon, and relay distances.
  • Stadtlauf Munchen (Half Marathon) - A popular city-center half marathon that winds through Munich's historic streets, past Marienplatz, and through green corridors. A fast, well-organized race that attracts both locals and visitors.
  • B2Run Munich (Corporate Run) - Munich's largest corporate running event, where thousands of employees from Bavarian companies run together in a festive team atmosphere. Held in Olympiapark with a lively post-race celebration.
  • Spartan Race Munich - An obstacle course race held in the Munich area, challenging runners with a mix of trail running and physical obstacles. A great option for those seeking something beyond traditional road races.
  • parkrun Englischer Garten (Every Saturday) - A free, timed 5 km run every Saturday morning in the Englischer Garten. Open to walkers and elite runners alike, parkrun is one of the best ways to connect with Munich's local running community each week.
  • Munchen lauft Series - A collection of shorter city races held throughout the year, ranging from 5 km to 10 km. These events are beginner-friendly and offer a taste of racing in different Munich neighborhoods and parks.

Beyond organized races, clubs like Munich Road Runners and the Munich Running Crew host free weekly group runs, and international communities on CorrerJuntos make it easy to find English-speaking training partners year-round.

When to Run in Munich: Seasonal Guide

Munich's Alpine-influenced climate offers four distinct seasons, each bringing a different running experience:

  • Spring (March - May) - Temperatures rise from 5 to 22 degrees as the city shakes off winter. April and May bring blooming magnolias in the Englischer Garten and longer daylight hours. Spring is one of the best seasons to run in Munich, with comfortable conditions and the parks coming alive. Rain showers are possible but rarely last long.
  • Summer (June - August) - Warm days between 20 and 28 degrees with long daylight hours extending past 9 pm. Summer running in Munich is excellent early in the morning or in the evening, as afternoon temperatures can climb and thunderstorms occasionally roll in from the Alps. The Isar River trails offer cooling shade, and many runners combine their sessions with a dip in the river afterwards.
  • Autumn (September - November) - Munich's golden season for running. Temperatures range from 5 to 18 degrees, the Englischer Garten turns spectacular with autumn foliage, and the Munich Marathon in October provides peak racing energy. Crisp mornings and stable weather make this the ideal time for marathon training blocks and long runs.
  • Winter (December - February) - Cold conditions from -5 to 5 degrees, but Munich winters have a special charm for runners. On clear days, the snow-capped Alps form a dramatic backdrop visible from Olympiaberg and the Isar bridges. Paths in the Englischer Garten are maintained, and the festive atmosphere of Christmas markets in December adds motivation for post-run rewards. Layer up properly and Munich's winter running can be surprisingly rewarding.
