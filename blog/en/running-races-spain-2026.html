Why 2026 is the year for races in Spain

Running in Spain is having a golden moment. Participation numbers break records year after year, and 2026 is no exception. The Valencia Marathon Trinidad Alfonso has been ranked one of the fastest in the world, the Behobia-San Sebastián sells out bibs in hours, and races like the San Silvestre Vallecana gather more than 40,000 runners.

What makes the Spanish calendar special is the variety: from urban races in Madrid and Barcelona to mountain trails in the Pyrenees and the Canary Islands, plus charity races, night events and festive 10Ks. There is a race for every runner, regardless of your level. And if you are visiting Spain or living here as an expat, racing is one of the best ways to experience local culture and meet the running community.

Tip: Plan your season around 2-3 target races and use the rest as training. Registration for the most popular events sells out quickly — don't wait until the last moment.

Tips for foreign runners racing in Spain

If you're traveling to Spain to race or living here as an expat, these are the things you need to know to register without surprises:

  • Federative license: many races require an "RFEA" license to participate. If you don't have one, you can buy a one-day license at registration for 3-5€. No medical certificate required.
  • Medical certificate: required for marathons and some half marathons. A simple doctor's letter saying you're fit for endurance sport is enough — doesn't need to be in Spanish, English works.
  • Bib pickup: usually 1-2 days before the race at a designated venue. Bring your passport. Some races allow third-party pickup if you give them a signed authorization.
  • ID at the start line: not always required, but many races check ID for prizes and age categories. Bring a copy in your phone.
  • Hotels sell out fast: book accommodation as soon as you register. Race weekends can triple normal prices.
  • Transport: most major Spanish races provide free public transport on race day with your bib. Confirm in the official regulations.
Pro tip: Spanish races require online registration in advance — same-day registration is rare. Mark deadlines in your calendar and pay attention to early bird windows (usually 30-50% cheaper).

How to pick the right race for you

With 24+ races to choose from, picking your target depends on three factors:

1. Your level and goal

  • First-timer or casual runner: stick to 10K races. Great vibes, low pressure, ~12 weeks of preparation.
  • Looking for a personal best: choose flat fast courses — Valencia (10K, half, marathon), Seville Half, Málaga Marathon.
  • Trail running fan: Transvulcania, Penyagolosa, Zegama-Aizkorri, Val d'Aran by UTMB.
  • Experience the local culture: Behobia-San Sebastián, Cursa de la Mercè Barcelona, San Silvestre Vallecana.

2. The time of year

  • Spring (April-May): ideal temperatures, longer training options. Best season for first marathons.
  • Summer (Jul-Aug): avoid road races, focus on mountain trail in the Pyrenees or Northern Spain.
  • Autumn (Oct-Nov): peak season. Best weather and most quality races.
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): cooler temperatures, perfect for fast times.

3. Your travel preferences

Coastal cities (Valencia, Barcelona, Málaga, Palma) work well as combined running + holiday trips. Inland cities (Madrid, Zaragoza, Seville) offer pure cultural immersion. Mountain races (Pyrenees, Canary Islands) require specific logistics — rent a car, prepare for altitude.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best marathon to run in Spain in 2026?

The Valencia Marathon Trinidad Alfonso is widely regarded as the best marathon in Spain and one of the five fastest in the world. Its flat course, ideal December weather and impeccable organization make it the favorite for personal bests.

How much does it cost to register for a popular race in Spain?

Prices vary by distance: 10K races usually cost 10€ to 25€, half marathons 25€ to 50€, and marathons 50€ to 90€. Early bird registrations are typically cheaper.

When do registrations open for popular races in Spain 2026?

Usually 6 to 12 months before the event. Major marathons (Valencia, Barcelona, Madrid) open in January-February and sell out within days or weeks. Popular 10K races usually open 2-3 months before.

Can foreign runners participate in Spanish popular races?

Yes, all major Spanish races welcome international runners. You only need a valid passport or ID and to complete the online registration form. Some races require a federative license (cheap to obtain at the same registration).

What are the best trail running races in Spain?

Spain is a world powerhouse in trail running. The most prestigious are Zegama-Aizkorri (42K with 2,750m elevation), Transvulcania (up to 73K), Val d'Aran by UTMB (up to 105K) and Penyagolosa Trails (up to 106K).

How do I prepare for my first race in Spain?

For a 10K, you need at least 8 weeks of preparation running 3-4 days per week. For a half marathon, plan 12 weeks, and for a marathon 16-20 weeks. Don't jump distances without proper preparation, and arrive at the race well rested.

What is the largest race in Spain?

The Zurich Rock 'n' Roll Madrid is the largest race in Spain by participation, with around 47,000 runners. The San Silvestre Vallecana on December 31 is also massive, with about 40,000 runners gathered for the iconic New Year's Eve 10K.
