Impact levels: why they matter

Sports bras are classified by how much movement they absorb. Picking the wrong level is the number-one cause of pain, chafing and reduced performance.

LevelActivitiesMovement reductionExample
Low impactYoga, pilates, walking~40%Basic wire-free top
Medium impactStationary bike, elliptical, ballet~60%Top with removable underwire
High impactRunning, HIIT, jumping, crossfit~75-80%Shock Absorber, Triumph Triaction
Important for runners: do not use medium-impact bras for running, even at slow jogging pace. The continuous vibration of running exceeds any static measurement and requires a high-impact bra specifically designed for this activity.

How to choose by cup size

There is no universal sports bra. The choice depends on cup size, training volume and predominant activity type.

Small breasts (AA-B)

You need high support but can go with lighter, less structured designs. Look for compressive models (which compress against the chest) rather than encapsulated (individual cups). With smaller breasts the movement is lower and a light, breathable model like the Nike Swoosh will be enough.

Medium breasts (C-D)

You need bras with hybrid technology (compression + individual cups). Winners in this range are the Under Armour Infinity High and the Adidas FastImpact. Adjustable straps and wide back band are essential features.

Larger breasts (DD+)

Mandatory: a bra with individual encapsulated cups, back closure with 3+ hooks and wide adjustable straps. The Shock Absorber Active Multi Sports and the Triumph Triaction Fitness F are the two absolute references. Investing 40-55€ in one of these instead of saving with cheap generics saves you from pain, breast tissue injury and discomfort.

Clinical note: repeated uncontrolled breast movement during physical activity produces micro-injuries to the Cooper's ligaments (connective tissues that maintain breast shape). These ligaments do not regenerate. An adequate high-impact sports bra reduces vertical movement by up to 75% and protects these ligaments.

Sizing and fit guide

How to measure your size correctly

80% of women wear the wrong bra size according to Triumph and Berlei studies. For running the fit must be precise to the millimeter: too big allows bounce; too small compresses excessively and cuts circulation.

  1. Under-bust measurement: with a tape measure, just under the breast tissue. This determines the band number (e.g. 85 cm → band 85 or 34 US).
  2. Over-nipple measurement: with a comfortable bra on, tape measure over the most prominent part of the breast.
  3. Difference between measurements = cup size: 12-14 cm = B, 15-17 cm = C, 18-20 cm = D, 21-23 cm = DD, 24-26 cm = E, etc.
Quick check: the under-bust band should sit horizontal on the back, not rise or fall. If it rises on the back, the band size is too large.

Signs your bra doesn't fit well

  • Band shifts up the back while running
  • You feel breast bounce during fast strides
  • Straps fall off or dig into shoulders leaving marks
  • Deep red marks under the breast when taking it off
  • You feel chafing in armpit or sternum
  • Cups wrinkle or form "double breast" overflow

Common mistakes when buying a running sports bra

  • Trusting XS-XL standard sizing without measuring: brand correspondence varies a lot. An XS Nike may equal an XS Under Armour but equal an S Adidas.
  • Choosing by aesthetics alone: a pretty but ill-fitting bra ruins sessions and accelerates breast tissue damage.
  • Not replacing it when elasticity is lost: a sports bra gradually loses support. After 50-80 washes its impact absorption capacity drops 30%. Even if it looks intact, it no longer supports the same.
  • Using a single sports bra rotated daily: having 2-3 in rotation extends their useful life. Elastane needs 24h to recover tension between uses.
  • Washing in the machine with other clothes and regular detergent: chlorine and softeners degrade elastane. Hand-wash with neutral soap or delicate cycle in mesh bag.
  • Sharing same size between brands: a Triumph 85C doesn't necessarily equal a Shock Absorber 85C. Try each brand individually.

Frequently asked questions about running sports bras

Why can't I use a regular bra for running?

Because they don't absorb enough impact. Everyday bras are designed for static support, not dynamic. In running the breast moves vertically up to 14 cm per stride — movement that causes permanent micro-injuries to Cooper's ligaments. A high-impact sports bra reduces that movement 75%.

How often should I replace my sports bra?

Every 50-80 washes or 6-12 months of regular use. Elastane progressively loses support capacity. Replacement signs: band no longer fits tight on the first hook, straps have lost recovery, or you feel more bounce than usual.

Signs my bra is too small or too big?

Too small: band deeply marks skin, breast overflows out of cups ("double breast"), difficulty breathing deeply. Too big: band rises on the back while running, cups wrinkle, straps fall.

How to wash a sports bra without damaging it?

By hand with neutral soap and cold water (best) or in a washing machine in mesh bag with delicate program and mild detergent. NEVER softener (degrades elastane), NEVER tumble dryer (deforms cups), NEVER bleach. Dry flat, not hanging from straps.

Compressive or encapsulated bra for running?

Small breasts (AA-B): compressive is enough (compresses against chest). Medium breasts (C-D): hybrid or encapsulated with compression. Larger breasts (DD+): mandatory encapsulated with individual cups — compression alone doesn't control movement in larger sizes.

Is it worth investing in a Shock Absorber or Triumph over a generic €15?

Depends on training volume. If you run 2-3 times per week in short sessions, a basic €15-20 option works fine. If you run 4+ times per week or do long runs (1h+), investing in a Shock Absorber or Triumph pays off in comfort, support and durability. At 2 years the cost per session comes out equal or cheaper.

Can I run without a bra if I have very small breasts?

Technically yes, but not recommended. Even with AA size there's enough movement to cause long-term micro-injuries to Cooper's ligaments. A light compression bra (Nike Swoosh, any basic high-impact option) is enough and very cheap. Running without a bra is a false economy.

Do front-zip bras hold as well as traditional ones?

Yes, when the zipper is properly designed (Under Armour Infinity, Shock Absorber Zip). They have internal reinforcement parallel to the zipper that distributes tension. Very practical for larger breasts where pulling a bra over the head is complicated. Confirm the brand specifies "high support" or "high impact".

Conclusion

A sports bra is the most critical piece of equipment for a female runner — above shoes, GPS watch or any other item. An adequate model protects your long-term breast health, prevents pain during training and lets you focus on running without constant discomfort.

If we could only recommend one, it would be the Shock Absorber Active Multi Sports. For larger breasts (D+), the Triumph Triaction Fitness F is unbeatable. For tight budget, a basic high-impact option covers the essentials for 15-20€.

Invest well here and you'll not only improve your running experience: you'll take care of your Cooper's ligaments — tissue that does not regenerate once damaged. Your 60-year-old self will thank you.
