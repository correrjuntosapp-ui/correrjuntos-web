Brussels' International Running Scene

Brussels offers runners excellent green spaces from urban parks to ancient forests. Bois de la Cambre provides peaceful escape minutes from downtown, whilst the Forêt de Soignes offers vast beech forest trails. CorrerJuntos connects you with Brussels' highly international running community serving the EU and NATO headquarters.

Whether you're training for the Brussels Marathon in October, the famous 20km de Bruxelles race, or exploring Parc du Cinquantenaire's elegant paths, Brussels combines European charm with serious running infrastructure. Find your multilingual crew and experience Belgian running culture.

Ancient Forest Trails

Best Neighbourhoods for Running in Brussels

Each Brussels commune offers a distinct running personality. These are the top areas to lace up and explore the Belgian capital on foot:

Ixelles & Bois de la Cambre

Ixelles is the heartland of Brussels running. Runners here enjoy instant access to Bois de la Cambre, the sprawling urban park with a central lake, wooded paths, and direct entry into Forêt de Soignes. The neighbourhood's lively cafes along Avenue Louise and Place Flagey make it perfect for post-run recovery. Many running groups gather at the park entrance on Avenue Franklin Roosevelt for morning and evening sessions.

EU Quarter & Parc du Cinquantenaire

The European Quarter centres around the magnificent Parc du Cinquantenaire, whose grand triumphal arch and wide gravel paths create a stunning backdrop for running. The park offers a popular 2 km loop that expat runners use for intervals and group meetups. After work, diplomats, EU officials, and international professionals fill the paths, making this Brussels' most multilingual running hub.

Uccle & Forêt de Soignes

Uccle is the gateway to Forêt de Soignes, the ancient 4,400-hectare beech forest that stretches across southern Brussels. Trail runners love Uccle for its quiet residential streets that quickly transition into UNESCO-protected woodland with soft forest paths, rolling terrain, and cathedral-like beech canopies. Weekend long runs here can easily reach 20 km or more without retracing your steps.

Woluwe-Saint-Pierre & Woluwe Parks

The Woluwe communes in east Brussels offer a green corridor of interconnected parks including Parc de Woluwe, Parc du Val Duchesse, and the Etangs Mellaerts lakes. The terrain is gently rolling with mixed surfaces from tarmac to grass and forest trail. It is quieter than the central parks, making it ideal for runners seeking peaceful, scenic routes away from the crowds.

Canal Area & Molenbeek

The Canal de Charleroi towpath has become one of Brussels' most popular flat running routes. Starting near Quai des Charbonnages and heading south, runners enjoy uninterrupted kilometres along the water with no traffic lights. The canal area is undergoing rapid regeneration with new bridges, cultural spaces, and improved paths, making it an increasingly vibrant spot for the Brussels running community.

Running Events and Races in Brussels

Brussels hosts a packed calendar of running events throughout the year. These are the essential races for every Brussels runner:

  • 20 km de Bruxelles (May) - The most iconic Brussels race, attracting over 40,000 participants. The course winds through tunnels, past the Royal Palace, through Bois de la Cambre and finishes at the Parc du Cinquantenaire. A must-run event for anyone living in the capital.
  • Brussels Marathon & Half Marathon (October) - The full 42.195 km marathon and 21.1 km half marathon take runners through the city centre, European quarter, and green spaces. A flat, fast course popular with runners chasing personal bests.
  • Belfius Brussels Night Run - A unique evening race through illuminated Brussels streets, combining sport with the atmosphere of the city after dark. Popular with casual and competitive runners alike.
  • Forêt de Soignes Trail Races - Multiple trail events throughout the year in the ancient beech forest, ranging from 10 km to ultra distances. Soft forest paths, rolling hills, and stunning woodland scenery.
  • parkrun Bois de la Cambre (every Saturday) - Free, timed 5 km every Saturday at 9:00 in Bois de la Cambre. Open to all levels from walkers to elite runners. A great way to meet Brussels' international running community.
  • Brussels Urban Trail - A unique race that takes runners through buildings, parks, and hidden passages across the city, combining running with cultural discovery.
  • Corrida de Noël (December) - The festive year-end race through central Brussels, often with costumed runners celebrating the holiday season.

Beyond organised races, Brussels has active chapters of Nike Run Club, Adidas Runners, and dozens of local clubs organising free group runs every week across the city.

When to Run in Brussels: Seasonal Guide

Brussels has a temperate maritime climate with running possible year-round. Each season brings its own character to the city's parks and forests:

  • Spring (April - June) - The best season for running in Brussels. Temperatures range from 10 to 18°C with increasing daylight. Bois de la Cambre and Forêt de Soignes burst into green, and the famous 20 km de Bruxelles in May makes this peak training season. Rain showers are common but brief.
  • Summer (July - August) - Pleasantly mild at 16-24°C, much cooler than Mediterranean cities. Long evenings allow for relaxed post-work runs. Forêt de Soignes provides welcome shade on warmer days. The international community thins out during August holidays, making parks quieter.
  • Autumn (September - October) - A spectacular season in Brussels. The beech forests of Soignes turn golden, temperatures cool to 8-16°C, and the Brussels Marathon in October gives runners a target race. Daylight shortens, so headlamps become essential for evening runs by late October.
  • Winter (December - February) - Cold and wet with temperatures between 2 and 7°C. Rain is frequent, and occasional snow dusts the forest trails. Days are short, but Brussels runners embrace it with proper layering and reflective gear. Winter running groups provide essential motivation during the dark months.
