How to choose your running bottle

The ideal hydration system depends on distance, heat, and your preferences. Key criteria:

  • Type: handheld bottle (5-15 km), hydration belt (10-21 km), soft flask in vest (trail/ultra). For short workouts, a handheld is simplest.
  • Capacity: 250-350 ml for 30-60 min workouts. 500 ml for long runs. Over 500 ml, consider a belt or vest.
  • Grip: running handhelds have adjustable straps. You shouldn’t have to squeeze to hold it.
  • Insulation: stainless steel bottles keep water cold 6-12 hours. Great for summer but heavier.
  • Nozzle: bite valves let you drink without opening/closing. More practical for running.
Tip: if you run less than 10 km, you often don’t need to carry water (drink before and after). Beyond 45 minutes or in heat, a 250-500 ml handheld is enough for most workouts.
