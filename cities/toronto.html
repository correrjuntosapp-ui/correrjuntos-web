Toronto's Vibrant Running Community

Toronto offers runners incredible diversity in both terrain and community. From the rolling hills of High Park to the flat Martin Goodman Trail along Lake Ontario's shore, the city provides routes for every training style. CorrerJuntos connects you with Toronto's welcoming, multicultural running community that runs strong through all four seasons.

Whether you're training for the Waterfront Marathon in October, exploring the Don Valley Trail system, or joining group runs in the Beaches neighborhood, Toronto's running scene welcomes all paces and abilities. Find your crew and discover why Torontonians run year-round, even through snowy winters.

Waterfront Running

Run the Martin Goodman Trail stretching over 20km along Lake Ontario, offering flat, scenic routes with skyline views and beach access points throughout.

Park & Trail Networks

Explore High Park's hilly terrain, the extensive Don Valley Trail system through ravines, or discover hidden gem routes through Toronto's ravine network.

Island Escapes

Ferry to Toronto Islands for car-free running with spectacular city skyline views, beaches, and peaceful paths just minutes from downtown.

Popular Running Areas in Toronto

  • High Park - Hilly 5km+ loops with trails and paved paths
  • Don Valley Trail - Extensive ravine trail system through the city
  • Martin Goodman Trail - 20km+ waterfront path along Lake Ontario
  • Toronto Islands - Car-free island routes with city views

Major Running Events

The Scotiabank Toronto Waterfront Marathon in October is Canada's premier marathon, offering stunning lakefront views and fast times. Toronto hosts numerous parkrun locations every Saturday, and clubs like the Longboat Toronto Roadrunners and High Park Running Club organize regular group training. The city's running scene stays active year-round, with winter running groups embracing the cold.
