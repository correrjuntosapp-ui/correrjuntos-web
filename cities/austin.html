Austin's Vibrant Trail Running Culture

Austin offers runners exceptional trail networks and year-round warm weather. The Lady Bird Lake Trail provides a 10-mile loop around downtown, whilst Barton Creek Greenbelt offers rugged limestone trail running. CorrerJuntos connects you with Austin's friendly, laid-back running community that embraces Texas heat.

Whether you're training for the Austin Marathon in February, the Capitol 10K in April, exploring Zilker Park's varied terrain, or discovering Shoal Creek's urban greenway, Texas's capital combines outdoor accessibility with weird, wonderful running culture. Find your crew and run like a true Austinite.

Lady Bird Lake Trail

Run the iconic 10-mile loop circling downtown Austin with skyline views, creek crossings, and constant runner traffic on the city's signature route.

Barton Creek Greenbelt

Explore 7.9 miles of rugged limestone trails through Hill Country terrain with creek crossings, swimming holes, and challenging technical running.

Urban Greenways & Parks

Discover Zilker Park's central location with trails and open spaces, or run Shoal Creek's urban greenway connecting North Austin to downtown.

Popular Running Areas in Austin

  • Lady Bird Lake Trail - 10-mile downtown loop, Austin's running mecca
  • Barton Creek Greenbelt - 7.9 miles of Hill Country trails
  • Zilker Park - Central park with varied running terrain
  • Shoal Creek - Urban greenway trail through the city

Major Running Events

The Austin Marathon in February offers perfect training weather and Hill Country scenery. The Capitol 10K in April is Texas's largest 10k. parkrun locations provide free weekly 5ks, and groups like Austin Runners Club organize regular training. Austin's running culture emphasizes early morning starts to beat the Texas heat.
