Berlin's Dynamic Running Scene

Berlin's running culture reflects the city itself: diverse, energetic, and filled with history. From the vast expanse of Tiergarten to the unique open space of former Tempelhof Airport, Berlin offers runners unmatched variety. CorrerJuntos connects you with the city's vibrant international running community.

Whether you're chasing a personal best at the legendary Berlin Marathon or exploring the 160km Mauerweg trail that follows the former Berlin Wall, there's a running group ready to join you. Berlin's flat terrain and extensive green spaces make it a runner's paradise year-round.

Urban Green Spaces

Explore Tiergarten's 5km loop through Berlin's central park, or enjoy the unique experience of running on Tempelhofer Feld's former airport runways.

Historic Routes

Follow the Mauerweg trail along the former Berlin Wall path, combining fitness with powerful historical reflection across 160 kilometers.

Forest Trail Running

Escape to Grunewald's extensive forest trails west of the city, or explore Treptower Park's riverside paths along the Spree.

Popular Running Areas in Berlin

  • Tiergarten - Central 5km loop through historic park with monuments
  • Tempelhofer Feld - Former airport, now 355 hectares of open running space
  • Mauerweg - 160km historic Berlin Wall trail around the city
  • Grunewald - Forest trails, lakes, and hill training opportunities
  • Treptower Park - Scenic riverside running along the Spree

Best Neighborhoods for Running in Berlin

Each Berlin neighborhood offers its own character and running experience. These are the top areas for runners in the German capital:

Tiergarten / Mitte

The heart of Berlin running. Tiergarten is a 210-hectare park in the city center offering a classic 5 km loop past the Victory Column, Bellevue Palace, and tree-lined avenues. Mitte surrounds it with flat, wide streets perfect for warm-up jogs past the Brandenburg Gate and along Unter den Linden. This is where most running groups meet and where the Berlin Marathon passes through its most iconic kilometers.

Kreuzberg

Kreuzberg is Berlin's creative hub with excellent canal-side running along the Landwehrkanal. The towpaths offer flat, car-free stretches through one of the city's most vibrant districts. You can run from Kottbusser Tor along the water to Tiergarten, linking neighborhoods seamlessly. After your run, Kreuzberg's cafes and food scene make it a favorite for social running groups.

Prenzlauer Berg

This leafy, family-friendly neighborhood in former East Berlin features Volkspark Friedrichshain, one of the city's oldest parks with rolling hills, tree-shaded paths, and a popular 2.5 km loop around the Fairy Tale Fountain. The Mauerpark area adds extra green space, and the flat streets of Prenzlauer Berg are ideal for easy recovery runs past charming pre-war architecture.

Charlottenburg / Grunewald

Charlottenburg provides the gateway to Grunewald, Berlin's largest forest at over 3,000 hectares. Trail runners can explore endless dirt paths, run around the Schlachtensee and Krumme Lanke lakes, or tackle the Teufelsberg hill for elevation training. The mix of forest trails and lakeside paths makes this area Berlin's best destination for off-road running and long weekend adventures.

Neukolln / Tempelhof

Neukolln borders Tempelhofer Feld, the former Tempelhof Airport converted into a massive 355-hectare public park. The flat, open perimeter offers a 6 km loop with wide runways, unobstructed views, and constant wind exposure that simulates race-day conditions. It is one of the most unique running venues in Europe and a favorite spot for speed work, long runs, and marathon preparation.

Major Running Events in Berlin

Berlin's race calendar ranks among the best in the world. The city hosts events for every distance and ambition throughout the year:

  • Berlin Marathon (September) - One of the six World Marathon Majors and arguably the fastest marathon course on the planet. The flat route through Tiergarten, past the Reichstag, and finishing at the Brandenburg Gate has produced multiple world records. Over 45,000 runners participate each year.
  • Berlin Half Marathon (April) - A fast spring half marathon through the city center, attracting thousands of runners kicking off their race season on Berlin's flat streets.
  • S25 Berlin (May) - A popular 25 km race that serves as an excellent bridge between half marathon and marathon training, held on a fast city-center course.
  • Berliner Firmenlauf (Corporate Relay) - Berlin's biggest corporate running event where companies field relay teams, combining team building with competitive running in a festive atmosphere.
  • Mauerweglauf (100-Mile Wall Trail Race) - An ultra-endurance event following the 160 km Mauerweg path along the former Berlin Wall. One of Europe's most unique ultra races, combining extreme distance with powerful history.
  • parkrun Berlin (Every Saturday) - Free, timed 5 km runs every Saturday morning at multiple locations across Berlin. Open to all levels from walkers to competitive runners. A great way to connect with the local running community each week.

Beyond major races, adidas Runners Berlin and Nike Run Club host free weekly group runs across the city, and dozens of local running clubs organize training sessions and social events year-round.

When to Run in Berlin: Seasonal Guide

Berlin's continental climate means four distinct seasons, each offering a different running experience:

  • Spring (March - May) - Temperatures climb from 5 to 22 degrees, cherry blossoms fill Tiergarten and the canal banks, and the city comes alive after winter. April and May are ideal months for running, with comfortable conditions and lengthening days. Half marathon season kicks off in spring.
  • Summer (June - August) - Warm days between 20 and 30 degrees with daylight lasting until nearly 10 pm. Long summer evenings are perfect for extended group runs through Tiergarten or along the Spree. Hydrate well on hot days and take advantage of the many water fountains in Berlin's parks.
  • Autumn (September - November) - Marathon season arrives with temperatures between 5 and 15 degrees. Golden leaves cover Grunewald and Tiergarten, creating spectacular running scenery. September and October combine mild weather with the peak energy of Berlin's biggest race events.
  • Winter (December - February) - Cold conditions from -5 to 5 degrees, but Berlin in winter has its own atmospheric charm. Run past Christmas markets in December, experience Tempelhofer Feld dusted in snow, or brave a frosty morning loop through Tiergarten. Fewer crowds on the paths and crisp air make winter running surprisingly rewarding for those who layer up properly.
