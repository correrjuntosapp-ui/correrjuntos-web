Valencia has become one of Spain's premier running destinations. With its Mediterranean climate, the spectacular Turia Gardens, and a thriving community of runners, the city offers unbeatable conditions for group running. If you are looking for running groups in Valencia, this comprehensive guide will help you find the one that best fits your level, schedule, and goals.

According to a study published in the British Journal of Sports Medicine (PubMed, 2015), group physical activity improves exercise adherence by up to 65% compared to training alone. In Valencia, this trend is reflected in the exponential growth of clubs, meetups, and running communities in recent years. Whether you are a complete beginner or preparing for the Valencia Marathon, there is a group waiting for you.

Valencia: The Perfect City for Group Running

It is no coincidence that Valencia ranks among the most popular cities for runners in Spain. The combination of climate, infrastructure, and running culture makes it a paradise for those who want to run with others in Valencia. The city regularly appears in global rankings of the best places to run, and for good reason.

The Turia Gardens: 9 Km of Runner's Paradise

The former bed of the Turia River was transformed into a 9-kilometer linear park that crosses the city from west to east. It is, without question, the epicenter of running in Valencia. With paths that alternate between packed earth and asphalt, natural shade from centuries-old trees, water fountains every few hundred meters, and completely flat terrain, it offers ideal conditions for every type of group workout.

The Turia connects the Bioparc zoo to the City of Arts and Sciences, creating a unique route that accommodates intervals, long runs, and easy jogs alike. Because it is integrated into the urban fabric, it is easily accessible from any neighborhood in Valencia. Explore more running routes in our guide to the best running routes in Valencia.

La Malvarrosa and the Seafront Promenade

For those who prefer the sound of the sea while they run, the Malvarrosa seafront promenade offers 6 kilometers of pedestrian lane along the beach. It is especially popular for morning workouts with running groups seeking the ocean breeze and coastal atmosphere. During summer, many Valencia running groups move their sessions here to take advantage of cooler temperatures near the water. The route can be extended toward the Port of Valencia or Patacona beach for extra distance.

Privileged Climate and Running Culture

With over 300 sunny days per year and average temperatures around 18 degrees Celsius, Valencia allows outdoor training practically year-round. The city hosts the Valencia Marathon Trinidad Alfonso, widely considered one of the fastest marathons in the world thanks to its flat course and favorable weather conditions. This marathon tradition has generated a vibrant running culture, with dozens of active groups throughout the year. For tips on running in warmer months, see our guide on running in summer heat.

The Best Running Groups in Valencia

Valencia boasts a diverse selection of running groups for every level. From federated clubs with certified coaches to informal communities that meet for easy runs through the Turia, there are options for every type of runner. Here are the most notable ones.

Valencia Running Club

One of the most established clubs in the city. They offer structured workouts Monday through Saturday in the Turia Gardens, with groups organized by pace (from 6:30/km to sub-4:00/km). They have a certified coach, personalized training plans, and participate in major races across the Valencia region. Ideal for runners looking to improve their times with a serious methodology and a motivating atmosphere.

Meeting point: Puente de las Flores, Turia · Schedule: Mon-Wed-Thu 7:30 PM, Sat 9:00 AM

Runners Valencia

An open, welcoming community that accepts all levels, from beginners taking their first steps to veterans preparing ultramarathons. They organize free weekly workouts, Sunday long runs, and social activities like post-workout breakfasts. Their philosophy is that nobody runs alone: there is always someone to share kilometers with. They are especially active on social media, where they share photos, tips, and celebrate every member's achievements.

Meeting point: Torres de Serrano · Schedule: Tue-Thu 8:00 PM, Sun 8:30 AM

Nike Run Club Valencia

The sportswear brand organizes free weekly meetups in Valencia, led by certified pacers. Workouts vary between speed sessions, easy jogs, and long runs. No prior registration or commitment is required: simply show up and run. It is an excellent gateway into the world of group running, particularly for those who have not yet committed to a club. They often give away t-shirts and sports gear at special events.

Meeting point: Nike Store Valencia · Schedule: Wed 7:30 PM

Parkrun Valencia

Parkrun is a global phenomenon that arrived in Valencia and has become a Saturday morning staple. Every Saturday at 9:00 AM, dozens of runners (and walkers) gather to complete a timed 5-kilometer course completely free of charge. Your pace does not matter: participants range from those finishing in 18 minutes to those who walk it in 40. It is an inclusive, family-friendly event with an exceptional community spirit. After the run, many participants stay for breakfast together, forging friendships that go beyond the pavement. More information on the official Parkrun Spain website.

Meeting point: Turia Gardens (Palau de la Música section) · Schedule: Sat 9:00 AM

Free Running Meetups in Valencia

If you do not want to commit to a club or simply prefer to try before you decide, Valencia offers numerous free running meetups every week. They are the most accessible way to start finding people to run with in the city.

Parkrun Valencia: The Saturday Classic

We mentioned it above, but it deserves its own section. Parkrun Valencia is the quintessential free running meetup. Every Saturday at 9:00 AM, rain or shine, you will find a group of runners waiting in the Turia. You only need to register once on the Parkrun website, print your barcode, and show up. They time you automatically, and you can track your progress week by week. It is ideal for beginners working toward their first 5K and for experienced runners who want a timed Saturday shake-out.

Running Store Meetups

Several specialty running stores in Valencia organize weekly meetups open to everyone. Forum Sport, Decathlon Valencia, and local shops like 42K Running regularly publish their meetups on Instagram. These sessions typically cover 5-8 kilometers at a moderate pace, followed by product discounts or giveaways for attendees. It is a great way to meet runners from your area and, as a bonus, try out new running shoes and gear.

Best Routes for Group Running in Valencia

Choosing the right route is essential when running in a group. You need enough space for everyone to run comfortably, good lighting for evening workouts, access to water fountains, and preferably flat terrain so the group can maintain a consistent pace. Valencia meets all of these requirements and more.

Turia Gardens: The Star Route

The Turia Gardens is the most popular route among running groups in Valencia. Its 9 kilometers of linear park offer a flat, shaded course with multiple variations. Groups typically choose a section based on workout distance: the Bioparc to Torres de Serrano stretch (4.5 km) for shorter sessions, or the full route to the City of Arts and Sciences for long runs of 12-18 km (out and back). There are public water fountains every 500-800 meters, public restrooms at several points, and the terrain alternates between packed earth and asphalt, which is ideal for protecting your joints.

Malvarrosa Seafront Promenade

The seafront promenade offers 6 kilometers of completely flat, straight pedestrian lane, perfect for pace-controlled workouts with your group. The sea breeze regulates temperature in summer, and the open landscape allows you to maintain visual contact with all group members. It is especially popular for sunrise morning sessions and for interval training with clear visual reference points. From the Malvarrosa you can extend the route to the Port of Valencia or Patacona beach to add extra kilometers.

La Albufera: Nature and Trails

Just 10 km from Valencia's city center, La Albufera Natural Park offers trails through rice fields, dunes, and pine forests that are a perfect alternative for groups seeking variety and nature. The paths are unpaved and can be somewhat uneven, making them more suitable for groups with some experience. Circular routes of 8-15 km through the Devesa del Saler are spectacular, especially at sunset. It is ideal for those looking to get started with trail running without leaving the city.

Bioparc Area – Turia Headwaters

The start of the Turia, near the Bioparc, is a common meeting point for many Valencia running groups. Cabecera Park offers a circular loop of approximately 2 km with varied terrain (grass, dirt, asphalt) and a calm atmosphere, ideal for warm-ups and cool-downs. From here, groups typically begin their Turia runs with the advantage of nearby parking and good metro and bus connections. If you are still deciding what gear you need, check our basic running pack for beginners.

Group tip: If your group has more than 8 people, split into pace-based sub-groups before starting. The Turia has plenty of space, but running in a large pack can inconvenience pedestrians and cyclists. Set a regrouping point every 2-3 km so nobody gets lost and everyone enjoys the workout. How to Choose Your Running Group in Valencia

With so many options available, choosing the right running group in Valencia can feel overwhelming. The key is identifying exactly what you are looking for and trying groups until you find the right fit. Based on our experience at CorrerJuntos, these are the most important factors when making your decision.

Choose Based on Your Level

This is the single most important factor. Running with a group that is too fast is frustrating and can lead to injuries; running with one that is too slow kills motivation. Look for groups that organize pace-based sub-groups or that have a clear range of paces. If you are a beginner (pace above 6:30/km), Parkrun Valencia and running store groups are ideal. If you are preparing for a sub-3:30 marathon, look for coached clubs like Valencia Running Club. To find runners at your exact pace, use the CorrerJuntos matching system. For more on finding compatible partners, read our article on how to find running partners.

Consider Schedule and Location

There is no point in finding the perfect group if they train when you cannot make it or on the other side of the city. Most Valencia running groups train between 7:00 PM and 8:30 PM on weekdays (to catch the sunset light in winter) and between 8:00 AM and 10:00 AM on weekends. If you live in Ruzafa, look for groups that meet in the central section of the Turia. If you live near the beach, you might prefer a group that trains on the Malvarrosa.

Define Your Goals

Looking for company to jog three times a week is very different from preparing for the Valencia Marathon. Federated clubs affiliated with the Royal Spanish Athletics Federation (RFEA) offer structure, certified coaching, and training plans. Informal groups and app-based meetups from CorrerJuntos prioritize flexibility and the social component. Both options are valid; it depends on what you need at this stage of your running journey. Read more about staying motivated to run to find the approach that suits you.

The benefits of running in a group go far beyond performance: they improve your mental health, build accountability, and turn training into a social activity. If you are still deciding between running solo or with others, we recommend trying both for a few weeks before committing.

How to Start Your Own Running Group in Valencia

If you cannot find a group that fits your needs, the best option may be to create your own. In Valencia, with so many runners looking for company, starting a running group is easier than you might think. Here are the key steps to doing it successfully.

Step 1: Pick a Fixed Day, Time, and Meeting Point

Consistency is what separates a running group that thrives from one that dies after two weeks. Choose a day and time you can maintain every week without exception. Tuesdays and Thursdays at 7:30 PM and Saturdays at 9:00 AM are the most popular schedules in Valencia. For your meeting point, choose a location that is easy to find and well-connected by public transport: Torres de Serrano, Puente de las Flores, or the Palau de la Musica in the Turia are unbeatable options.

Step 2: Publish Your Meetup

Create the meetup on CorrerJuntos so runners in the area can see it on the map. Also share it in local WhatsApp running groups, on Instagram using hashtags like #runningvalencia and #correrporganasvalencia, and in running forums. The first few sessions may attract only 2-3 people; that is normal. Consistency will make the group grow organically week after week. For more tips, read about the best apps for group running.

Step 3: Organize by Pace

Once the group exceeds 6-8 people, start organizing pace-based sub-groups. One leader per sub-group is sufficient. Set an inclusive base pace (for example, 6:00/km for the main group) and allow faster runners to do their own block. Regroup at the end for stretching and conversation. This system allows the group to grow without anyone being left behind.

Step 4: Build Community Beyond the Run

Running groups that endure are those that build bonds outside of training. Organize post-workout breakfasts on Saturdays, sign up with the group for popular races like the 15K Valencia Abierta or the Volta a Peu, and create a WhatsApp group to share photos and motivate each other. The social dimension is what turns a training group into a real community. As many runners discover, group running is genuinely addictive. You can also learn about running safely with strangers to set ground rules from the start.

Expert tip: The best meeting points in Valencia for running groups are those with easy public transport access, high visibility (so newcomers can find the group), and space to warm up. Torres de Serrano and Puente de las Flores tick all of these boxes.

Frequently Asked Questions About Running Groups in Valencia

What are the best running groups in Valencia?

Among the standouts are Valencia Running Club (structured workouts in the Turia), Runners Valencia (open community for all levels), Nike Run Club Valencia (free weekly meetups), and Parkrun Valencia (free 5K every Saturday). You can also find active groups on the CorrerJuntos app filtered by area, level, and schedule.

Where can I run with a group in Valencia for free?

There are many free options: Parkrun Valencia organizes a 5K every Saturday in the Turia, stores like Forum Sport and Decathlon host weekly meetups, and the CorrerJuntos app helps you find free meetups near you. The Turia Gardens and the Malvarrosa seafront promenade are the most common meeting points.

Do I need experience to join a running group in Valencia?

Not at all. Most groups accept all levels, from beginners who alternate walking and running to experienced marathon runners. Good groups organize pace-based sub-groups so everyone can train comfortably. Parkrun Valencia is especially recommended for those just starting out, as there is zero pace pressure. For a confidence boost, read our guide on how to start running when you feel embarrassed.

What are the best routes for group running in Valencia?

The four most popular routes are: Turia Gardens (9 km, flat, shaded), Malvarrosa seafront promenade (6 km along the beach), La Albufera nature trails (8-15 km through rice fields and pine forests), and the Bioparc area at the start of the Turia. All are safe, well-lit, and have water fountains.

How much does it cost to join a running club in Valencia?

It depends on the type. Informal meetups, Parkrun, and apps like CorrerJuntos are completely free. Federated athletics clubs charge between 20 and 50 euros per month, which includes a certified coach, sports insurance, and race discounts. Many running stores also organize free weekly meetups.

How can I start my own running group in Valencia?

Pick a fixed day and time, choose an accessible meeting point in the Turia (like Torres de Serrano), post the meetup on CorrerJuntos or social media, and start with 3-4 people. Consistency is key: the group will grow organically if you keep the commitment every week.
