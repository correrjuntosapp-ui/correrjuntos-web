We analyzed the best magnesium supplements on the market to help you prevent cramps, improve your sleep and speed up muscle recovery.

Magnesium is the most underrated mineral in sports supplementation, and possibly the most important one for runners. It participates in over 300 enzymatic reactions in your body, including muscle contraction and relaxation, energy production (ATP), protein synthesis and nervous system regulation. The International Society of Sports Nutrition (ISSN) notes that endurance athletes lose magnesium through sweat in every session, placing them at risk of deficiency (World Athletics) (ACSM).

The symptoms of magnesium deficiency are exactly what a runner wants to avoid: muscle cramps, excessive fatigue, poor sleep, slow recovery and greater injury risk. In 2026 the market offers dozens of magnesium forms, but not all are absorbed equally or serve the same purpose. We have analyzed the 8 best magnesium supplements so you can find the ideal one for your training type and needs.

Types of magnesium: which one to choose

Not all magnesium supplements are created equal. The chemical form of the compound determines how much your body absorbs and what it is most useful for. These are the four most relevant forms for runners:

  • Bisglycinate (chelated with glycine): This is the form with the highest bioavailability, with absorption exceeding 80%. Glycine is an amino acid that promotes muscle relaxation and improves sleep quality. It causes no gastrointestinal discomfort. It is the best option for runners seeking optimal recovery and rest.
  • Magnesium citrate: Good bioavailability (around 30-40%) and a more affordable cost than bisglycinate. Well tolerated by most people. At high doses it may have a mild laxative effect, which some runners may notice. It is the second-best value-for-money option.
  • Magnesium oxide: The cheapest form but with very low absorption (only 4%). Despite having the highest concentration of elemental magnesium per capsule, your body barely utilizes a fraction. Not recommended for athletes seeking real results.
  • Magnesium L-Threonate: This is the only form that efficiently crosses the blood-brain barrier. Developed at MIT, it is especially indicated for improving cognitive function, concentration during long races and deep sleep quality. Its price is higher, but its neurological benefits are unique.
Tip: For most runners, magnesium bisglycinate is the best choice due to its high absorption and dual benefit (muscular + sleep). If your budget is tight, magnesium citrate is an excellent alternative. Avoid magnesium oxide: it is a waste of money. How to choose the right magnesium

Choosing the best magnesium for you depends on three main factors:

  • Your main problem: If you suffer from frequent cramps, prioritize bisglycinate or citrate for their good muscular absorption. If your issue is sleep or mental fatigue, L-Threonate is the best option. If you just want to maintain basic levels, any quality citrate will do.
  • Your stomach: Bisglycinate is the gentlest on the digestive system. Citrate can cause a mild laxative effect in some people, especially at first. Oxide is the harshest and worst tolerated. If you have a sensitive stomach, go straight for bisglycinate.
  • Your budget: Ana Maria Lajusticia and Nutravita are the most affordable options. HSN and Natural Elements offer premium bisglycinate at good prices. Doctor's Best and Life Extension are the premium choices with the best science behind them.
Editor's recommendation: For most runners, HSN Magnesium Bisglycinate is the best overall choice: maximum absorption, zero stomach issues, reliable Spanish brand and an unbeatable price. If you want an extra boost for sleep, combine with Life Extension L-Threonate at night.

Magnesium dosage for runners

The optimal magnesium dose depends on your activity level, body weight and how much you sweat during training. Here are the general recommendations:

  • Recreational runners (3-4 days/week): 300-350mg of elemental magnesium per day. One capsule of bisglycinate or citrate is usually enough.
  • Intermediate runners (5-6 days/week): 350-400mg per day. If you run long on weekends, increase to 400mg on higher volume days.
  • Marathon and ultra-trail runners: 400-500mg per day during high-volume training periods. Consider combining bisglycinate (day) + threonate (night).
  • Hot weather or excessive sweating: Add an extra 50-100mg on heavy sweating days. Magnesium is lost through sweat and on extreme heat days you can lose twice as much.

The best time to take magnesium is at night, 30-60 minutes before sleep. It promotes muscle relaxation and improves sleep quality, which is the most important recovery period for a runner. If you take more than one capsule, you can split them: one with lunch and another before bed. Avoid taking it right before training.

Important: Always look at the amount of elemental magnesium, not the total weight of the compound. A 500mg bisglycinate capsule may contain only 100mg of elemental magnesium. The products in this comparison indicate the dose in actual elemental magnesium. You need at least 4-6 weeks of continuous use to notice significant improvements in cramps and sleep quality.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best type of magnesium for runners?

Magnesium bisglycinate is the best type for runners. It has the highest bioavailability (over 80% absorption), causes no stomach issues and combines magnesium with glycine, an amino acid that promotes muscle relaxation and sleep. Magnesium citrate is the second-best option, with good absorption at a lower cost. Magnesium oxide has very low absorption (only 4%) and is not recommended for athletes.

How much magnesium does a runner need per day?

The recommended magnesium dose for runners is 300-400mg of elemental magnesium per day. Runners who train more than 5 days per week or do high-intensity sessions may need up to 500mg. Magnesium is lost through sweat during exercise, so runners need more than sedentary people. It is important to look at elemental magnesium, not the total weight of the compound.

When is the best time to take magnesium if you run?

The best time to take magnesium is at night, 30-60 minutes before bedtime. Magnesium promotes muscle relaxation and improves sleep quality, which is when your body recovers from training. If you prefer taking it during the day, do so with a meal to improve absorption and avoid stomach discomfort. Never take it right before training as it can cause drowsiness or digestive issues.

Does magnesium prevent cramps while running?

Yes, magnesium plays a fundamental role in preventing muscle cramps. Magnesium regulates muscle contraction and relaxation, and its deficiency is one of the most common causes of cramps in runners. Runners lose magnesium through sweat, especially during long workouts or in hot weather. Maintaining optimal magnesium levels significantly reduces the frequency and intensity of cramps.

Can you combine magnesium with other running supplements?

Yes, magnesium combines perfectly with most sports supplements. It is compatible with omega-3, whey protein, creatine, collagen and vitamin D. In fact, magnesium is necessary for vitamin D activation in the body. Avoid taking it at the same time as calcium or iron supplements as they compete for absorption. Leave at least 2 hours between taking magnesium and calcium or iron.
