Barcelona is, without question, one of the best cities in Europe for running. Year-round Mediterranean climate, kilometres of seafront promenade, mountains twenty minutes from the city centre and a running community that keeps growing every month. If you are looking for running groups in Barcelona, you have come to the right place.

In this guide we cover everything: the most active clubs, free weekly meetups, the best routes for group running and how to find runners at your level and in your area. Whether you are a complete beginner or training for a marathon, Barcelona has a group for you.

A study published in the British Journal of Sports Medicine found that group exercise significantly increases training adherence and reduces perceived stress levels. In Barcelona, with so many options available, there is no reason to run alone.

Barcelona, the social running capital of Spain

Barcelona is home to one of Spain's most active running communities, and that is no coincidence. The combination of mild weather, sport-friendly urban infrastructure and a packed calendar of popular races makes the city a paradise for runners of every level.

The numbers speak for themselves: the Zurich Barcelona Marathon attracts over 20,000 participants each year, the Cursa dels Bombers draws more than 14,000 runners, and the Cursa de El Corte Ingles is one of Europe's largest mass-participation races with over 50,000 entries. Barcelona lives and breathes running.

But beyond the races, what makes Barcelona truly special is its social running culture. Running in Barcelona happens on the streets, in the parks and along the beaches. Every evening, the Barceloneta promenade, Parc de la Ciutadella and the slopes of Montjuic fill with groups of runners at all paces. And every Saturday morning, hundreds of people gather for organised meetups before heading to brunch together.

The most popular zones for group running include:

  • Barceloneta and the Seafront Promenade: the most iconic circuit in the city, flat terrain with a refreshing sea breeze.
  • Montjuic: hills with elevation gain, staircases and panoramic views of the port and the city skyline.
  • Parc de la Ciutadella: a closed loop perfect for intervals and speed workouts.
  • Carretera de les Aigues: 10 km of shaded dirt trail with spectacular views across the entire city.
  • Diagonal Mar and Forum: a wide, modern area that is less crowded, ideal for long runs.

This combination of geographical variety and an active community makes Barcelona the perfect city to find your running group. And with the social running trend continuing to grow in 2026, there have never been more options than right now.

The best running groups in Barcelona

Barcelona has dozens of active running groups, from federated clubs with decades of history to informal communities born on Instagram. These are the standout options ranked by size, activity level and accessibility.

Barcelona Runners

One of the largest and most established running groups in the city. They organise weekly training sessions several days a week with pace sub-groups ranging from 7:00 min/km to sub-4:30. Very active on social media and with an inclusive atmosphere that blends abilities and nationalities. Usual meeting point: Ciutadella and Barceloneta.

Best for: Runners of all levels looking for a large, well-organised and social group.

We Run Barcelona

A community focused on fun and the social side of running. Their meetups combine training with post-run plans (breakfasts, beers, day trips). They organise monthly challenges and enter popular races together. Very active on Instagram with motivational content and a welcoming vibe.

Best for: Runners who value the social experience just as much as the training itself.

Nike Run Club Barcelona

NRC Barcelona organises free coached training sessions several times a week. Sessions typically include a dynamic warm-up, a running workout and a cool-down stretch. The coaches are professionals who adapt each session to different ability levels. Usual meeting point: Nike Store on Passeig de Gracia.

Best for: Runners who want structured, coach-led workouts at no cost.

adidas Runners Barcelona

Part of the global adidas Runners programme with an active presence in Barcelona. They combine running sessions with functional training, yoga and nutrition talks. International atmosphere with many expats in the group. Training sessions are usually on Tuesdays and Thursdays at sunset.

Best for: Runners seeking a holistic approach (running plus cross-training) in an international environment.

Parkrun Barceloneta

Part of the global Parkrun movement, which organises free, timed 5K runs open to all levels every Saturday at 9:00 AM on the Barceloneta seafront. No pre-registration or experience required. Simply register once online, print your barcode and show up. The atmosphere is festive and truly inclusive.

Best for: Anyone, from walkers to advanced runners. Perfect as a first taste of group running.

These are not the only options. Barcelona also has dozens of federated clubs registered with the Royal Spanish Athletics Federation (RFEA) and the Catalan Athletics Federation, such as Club Atletic Barcelona, CA Montjuic and Unio Atletica Terrassa. If you want structure, a coach and competitive racing, a federated club may be your best bet.

Free running meetups in Barcelona

You do not need to pay a membership fee to run with a group in Barcelona. There are multiple free options that operate every week, rain or shine.

Parkrun Barceloneta (Saturdays 9:00 AM)

The crown jewel of free running in Barcelona. Every Saturday, between 100 and 300 people gather on the seafront promenade to run a timed 5K. It is completely free, non-competitive (although times are recorded) and open to everyone: runners, walkers, families with pushchairs. The post-run atmosphere by the beach is unbeatable.

All you need to do is register once at parkrun.es and print your personal barcode. Bring it every Saturday and you are set. Volunteers scan your barcode at the finish line and you receive your results by email. It is a brilliant way to join a running group for the first time.

Running shop meetups

Several specialist running shops in Barcelona organise free weekly meetups as a community-building strategy. Among the most popular:

  • 42K Running (Eixample): guided weekday training sessions with different pace groups.
  • RunnersFast (Gracia): Wednesday meetups with routes that vary each week around the northern part of the city.
  • Forum Sport and Decathlon: occasional group running events, especially during marathon season.

These meetups are perfect if you want to try different groups with no commitment and discover shops where you can find running gear with expert advice.

Best routes for group running in Barcelona

Not all routes are equal when it comes to group running. You need enough width to run side by side, good lighting if you run at dusk, and ideally minimal traffic interruptions. These are the four best routes in Barcelona for running with a group. For a deeper look at the city's running infrastructure, check out our guide to the best running routes in Barcelona.

1. Barceloneta Seafront Promenade (5 km)

The quintessential Barcelona running circuit. From the W Hotel to the Port Olimpic (and back), you get a completely flat course with smooth pavement, full lighting at night and a sea breeze. This is where Parkrun takes place every Saturday and the meeting point for most of the larger groups. If you are running at night, the promenade is one of the safest options.

Profile: Flat, 5 km one way. Surface: Tarmac and tile. Best time: Sunrise or sunset.

2. Montjuic (8 km)

For those who crave elevation and views. The classic loop climbs from Placa Espanya through the Jardins de Laribal, circles the Castell de Montjuic and descends on the port side. There are serious ramps (some sections at 10-12% gradient), staircases and mixed paths. The views of the harbour, Tibidabo and the sea make every metre of climbing worthwhile. A great option if you enjoy uphill and downhill running technique.

Profile: Hilly, 200 m total elevation gain. Surface: Tarmac plus trail. Best time: Morning (shade on the eastern slopes).

3. Carretera de les Aigues (10 km)

Barcelona's best-kept running secret. A flat dirt path running along the mid-slope of the Collserola mountain range at around 300 metres altitude. Natural shade from pine trees, panoramic views of the entire city and zero cars. Easily accessible from the FGC train (Peu del Funicular station) or by running up from Vallvidrera. If you are drawn to off-road terrain, this is a perfect introduction to trail running.

Profile: Virtually flat (gentle undulations). Surface: Compacted dirt. Best time: Any time, but sunrise is magical.

4. Parc de la Ciutadella (3 km)

The ideal loop for intervals, speed sessions and structured group workouts. The perimeter circuit of the park measures roughly 1.5 km and is completely flat with good pavement. Many groups use it as a base for warm-ups before running repeats on the long straights inside the park. Very central and accessible by metro (Arc de Triomf or Ciutadella-Vila Olimpica). If you are working on running faster, this is your spot.

Profile: Flat. Surface: Tarmac plus park paths. Best time: Weekday evenings.

Summer tip for group running in Barcelona: From June to September, prioritise early morning slots (6:30-8:00) or late evening runs (19:30-21:00). Midday temperatures can exceed 30 degrees Celsius with high humidity. Carretera de les Aigues and Montjuic offer more shade than the coastal routes. Read more in our summer running guide.

How to choose your running group in Barcelona

With so many options available, choosing the right group can feel overwhelming. But if you ask yourself the right questions, the decision becomes much simpler. These are the key criteria to evaluate.

By level and pace

This is the single most important factor. Running with people who are significantly faster or slower than you is frustrating for everyone. The best groups organise pace sub-groups. If you run at 6:00 min/km, look for a group that has a 5:45-6:15 sub-group. If you are just starting out and alternating between walking and jogging, look for groups specifically aimed at beginners. Our pace guide for beginners can help you figure out where you stand.

The CorrerJuntos Matching feature automatically connects you with runners at a similar pace. No need to ask around: the algorithm compares your pace, schedule and area with other runners in Barcelona and suggests compatible matches.

By schedule

There is no point finding the perfect group if they train on Tuesdays at 7:00 AM and you cannot make it before 7 PM. Before committing, verify that the schedule fits your actual routine. Larger groups tend to offer multiple weekly sessions, which gives you more flexibility. If you are unsure about the best time to run, experiment with a few different slots.

By location

Barcelona is a big city. A group that meets in the Eixample will not work for you if you live in Badalona and do not want to take the metro just to train. Look for groups that train near your home or workplace. The areas with the highest concentration of group running activity are:

  • Barceloneta / Vila Olimpica: the highest density of groups and meetups.
  • Eixample: central location with quick access to Ciutadella and Montjuic.
  • Gracia / Sarria: groups that tend to train on Carretera de les Aigues and in Collserola.
  • Poblenou / Diagonal Mar: a growing area with wide, modern routes.

By goal

Looking for a group to socialise is not the same as looking for one to prepare for a marathon. Some groups focus on competition and performance (federated clubs, interval training groups), while others prioritise fun and the social element (We Run Barcelona, brunch meetups). Be honest with yourself about what you really want. If you are new to group running, our guide on your first running meetup covers everything you need to know before your first day.

How to start your own running group in Barcelona

If you cannot find exactly what you are looking for, create it yourself. Starting a running group in Barcelona is easier than it sounds. All you need is a meeting point, a fixed schedule and two other people to get going.

Step 1: Choose a strategic meeting point

Your meeting point should be easy to find, accessible by public transport and have enough space for a warm-up. The best spots in Barcelona:

  • Main entrance of Parc de la Ciutadella (Passeig de Pujades side): L1/L4 metro, ample space, water fountains.
  • The golden fish sculpture (Port Olimpic): iconic, visible from a distance, right on the seafront promenade.
  • Font Magica de Montjuic: for hill routes, accessible via Espanya metro station.
  • Placa de les Glories: ideal if your group trains in the Poblenou/Diagonal Mar area.

Step 2: Set a regular, non-negotiable schedule

Consistency is the number one factor that determines whether a group survives. Pick a fixed day and time and never change it unless absolutely necessary. The schedules that work best in Barcelona:

  • Tuesdays and Thursdays 19:00: the classic weekday combination, after work.
  • Saturdays 9:00: the prime weekend slot, compatible with brunch afterwards.
  • Wednesdays 7:00: for early birds who prefer to run before work.

For more tips on warming up before running, make sure you build a brief dynamic warm-up into your group routine.

Step 3: Publish and promote your meetup

Create your first meetup on CorrerJuntos (it appears on the map for all users in Barcelona), share it on Instagram and WhatsApp, and tell your gym buddies or neighbours. The first 3-4 sessions are the hardest. After that, word of mouth does the rest. You can also check out the best apps for running groups to maximise your reach.

Step 4: Manage growth

When your group exceeds 8-10 people, it is time to organise pace sub-groups. Assign a pacer for each sub-group (someone who sets the rhythm and makes sure nobody gets left behind) and establish a regrouping point every 2-3 km. That way everyone runs comfortably without anyone being left alone. If you are concerned about safety when running with strangers, our guide covers all the precautions.

Key tip: The post-run social is just as important as the run itself. Groups that grab breakfast, have a drink or simply chat for 15 minutes after running have significantly higher retention rates. In Barcelona, the seafront terraces and the Eixample cafes make the perfect complement to a morning run.

Frequently asked questions about running groups in Barcelona

What are the best running groups in Barcelona?

The most popular groups include Barcelona Runners, We Run Barcelona, Nike Run Club Barcelona, adidas Runners Barcelona and Parkrun Barceloneta. You can also find local meetups and neighbourhood groups on the CorrerJuntos app.

Where can I run for free with a group in Barcelona?

Parkrun Barceloneta organises a free timed 5K every Saturday at 9:00 AM on the seafront. Running shops like 42K Running and RunnersFast host free weekly meetups. The CorrerJuntos app shows free meetups near you in real time.

What level do I need to join a running group in Barcelona?

No minimum level is required. Most groups welcome all abilities, from beginners who alternate walking and jogging to experienced marathon runners. Larger groups create pace sub-groups so everyone can run at a comfortable effort.

What are the best routes for group running in Barcelona?

The four standout routes are: the Barceloneta seafront promenade (5 km, flat), Montjuic (8 km, hilly), Carretera de les Aigues (10 km, shaded trail) and Parc de la Ciutadella (3 km, ideal for speed sessions).

How do I start my own running group in Barcelona?

Choose a central, accessible meeting point, set a fixed day and time, publish your meetup on CorrerJuntos and share it on social media. Start with 3-4 people and grow organically. Consistency is the key to long-term success.

When do running groups in Barcelona usually meet?

Most groups meet on weekday evenings (18:30-20:00) and weekend mornings (8:00-10:00). Tuesday and Thursday are the most popular weekdays. Saturday mornings see the biggest gatherings, including Parkrun at 9:00 AM. In summer, schedules shift earlier to beat the heat.
