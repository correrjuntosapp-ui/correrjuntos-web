But not just any backpack will do: you need one that doesn’t bounce, lets you access bottles without stopping, and ventilates enough to avoid becoming an oven.

How to choose a trail running pack

The perfect pack depends on the distance you run and the gear you need to carry. Here are the key criteria:

  • Capacity: 5-8 L for races up to mountain marathon. 10-15 L for ultras with extensive mandatory gear lists (rain jacket, emergency blanket, headlamp, whistle, collapsible cup...).
  • Vest-style fit: the best trail packs are actually vests that wrap around your torso. They don’t bounce, distribute weight evenly, and let you access everything without removing the pack.
  • Front pockets: essential for soft flasks. Being able to drink without stopping makes a real difference in competition.
  • Empty weight: under 300 g for vests, under 400 g for 10+ L packs. Every gram counts in the mountains.
  • Ventilation: breathable mesh on the back to prevent heat and sweat buildup.
Tip: for your first trail race, an 8 L vest like the Salomon Active Skin 8 covers 90% of needs. You only need 12+ L if you run ultras with extensive mandatory gear lists.
