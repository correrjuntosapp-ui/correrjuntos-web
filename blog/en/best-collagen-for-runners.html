Collagen is the most abundant structural protein in the human body: it represents 30% of all your body protein and is the main component of tendons, ligaments, cartilage and fascia. For a runner, these structures withstand an impact of between 2.5 and 3 times your body weight with every stride, subjecting them to progressive wear. After the age of 25-30, natural collagen production decreases by 1-1.5% per year, accelerating joint deterioration and the risk of injuries such as tendinopathies, chondromalacia or plantar fasciitis.

The good news is that supplementation with hydrolyzed collagen has been shown in clinical studies to reduce joint pain in athletes and improve collagen synthesis in tendons and ligaments. In 2026 the market offers dozens of options, but not all have the same quality, dosage or type of collagen. We have analyzed the 9 best collagen supplements for runners to help you find the ideal one for your type of training and your joint needs.

Types of collagen: which to choose

There are at least 28 types of collagen identified, but only three are truly relevant for runners. The way collagen is processed also determines how much your body absorbs and how it uses it:

  • Type I Collagen: Represents 90% of body collagen. It is the main component of tendons, ligaments, skin and bones. For runners it is the most important because it strengthens the structures that withstand the impact of every stride. Type I hydrolyzed collagen peptides have a bioavailability of over 90%.
  • Type II Collagen: Found mainly in articular cartilage. It is key for runners who already experience discomfort in knees, hips or ankles. Undenatured type II collagen (UC-II) has shown promising results in protecting articular cartilage even at small doses of 40mg per day.
  • Marine Collagen: Obtained from fish, it has a lower molecular weight than bovine, which theoretically improves its absorption. It is rich in type I collagen and is the preferred option for those who avoid bovine or porcine products. It may have a slight fishy taste in powder form.
  • Hydrolyzed Collagen (peptides): This is not a type, but a process. Collagen is broken down into small fragments (peptides) of 2,000-5,000 daltons that are absorbed directly in the intestine. It is the most bioavailable form and has the most scientific evidence in sports. Always look for the hydrolyzed label.
Tip: For most runners, type I hydrolyzed collagen is the best choice due to its high absorption and direct action on tendons and ligaments. If you already have joint wear in your knees, add type II collagen or choose a supplement that combines both. Always take collagen with vitamin C: it is essential for your body to synthesize new collagen. Find your training group on CorrerJuntos.

How to choose the right collagen

Choosing the best collagen for you depends on three main factors:

  • Your main goal: If you want to consume several grams of peptides for tendons and ligaments, prioritise type I hydrolyzed collagen (Colnatur, HSN or Weider). If you prioritise a cartilage-focused formula, AMIX supplies undenatured type II collagen UC-II®. If you want the largest hydrolyzed peptide serving, Vital Proteins provides 20g.
  • Your preferred format: Powder provides the largest hydrolyzed peptide servings and often good value. Capsules do not automatically mean an insufficient dose: 40 mg of UC-II® cannot be compared directly with 10g of hydrolyzed collagen. AMIX fits its directions into one daily capsule, while other tablet products may require several units.
  • Your budget: Ana Maria Lajusticia and HSN are the most affordable options. Colnatur and Epaplus offer complete formulas at a mid-range price. Vital Proteins and Weider are the premium options with the best raw materials.
Editor's recommendation: For most runners, HSN Hydrolyzed Collagen with Magnesium remains the best-value choice for its peptide dose, magnesium and vitamin C. For runners prioritising joint cartilage and a one-a-day capsule, AMIX ProFlex UC-II is the comparison's dedicated undenatured type II collagen option. These are different formats, and there is no universal choice for every goal.

When to take collagen for runners

Timing and dose depend on the format. Gram-based recommendations in this section refer to hydrolyzed collagen or collagen peptides; UC-II® is undenatured type II collagen and is used in much smaller quantities. It is not appropriate to compare 40 mg of UC-II® directly with 10g of hydrolyzed collagen.

  • 30-60 minutes before training: This is the option supported by the most studies. Taking collagen with vitamin C before exercise increases collagen synthesis in tendons and ligaments during physical activity. Exercise stimulates blood flow to these structures, facilitating the delivery of collagen amino acids.
  • On an empty stomach in the morning: If you don't train in the morning, taking collagen on an empty stomach optimizes absorption because it doesn't compete with other dietary proteins. Add a glass of water with lemon or orange juice to provide the necessary vitamin C.
  • Before bed: During deep sleep, the highest peaks of growth hormone occur, which stimulates collagen synthesis. Taking collagen before bed takes advantage of this natural anabolic window.
  • Recreational runners (3-4 days/week): 10g daily is sufficient. You can take it in a single dose before training or split between morning and night.
  • Marathoners and ultra-trail runners: 15-20g daily during high-volume periods. Split the dose: 10g before training and 5-10g before bed.
  • AMIX ProFlex UC-II: the official directions are one capsule per day between meals, taken with plenty of water. Each dose already provides 80 mg of vitamin C.

The benefits of collagen are not immediate: you need at least 8-12 weeks of continuous use to notice significant improvements in joint health and tendon strength. Consistency is more important than any single dose. Establish a daily routine and don't stop even if you don't notice changes in the first few weeks.

Important: Vitamin C is absolutely essential for collagen synthesis. Without vitamin C, your body cannot use the collagen peptides you ingest. If your supplement doesn't include vitamin C (like Vital Proteins or Nutravita in their basic formulas), add a natural orange juice, a kiwi or a 500mg vitamin C supplement along with your collagen.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best type of collagen for runners?

Hydrolyzed collagen type I and type II is the best for runners. Type I strengthens tendons, ligaments and skin, while type II protects articular cartilage. Hydrolyzed collagen peptides have a bioavailability of over 90%, meaning your body absorbs and uses them efficiently. For runners, the ideal supplement combines both types or provides high-purity hydrolyzed collagen peptides.

How much collagen does a runner need per day?

For hydrolyzed collagen peptides, commonly discussed servings are measured in grams and are often around 10-15g daily. UC-II® is undenatured type II collagen and is used in much smaller quantities: AMIX's 40 mg should not be compared directly with 10g of hydrolyzed peptides. Always follow the product label; ProFlex UC-II directs one capsule daily between meals.

When is the best time to take collagen if you run?

The best time to take collagen is 30-60 minutes before training, accompanied by vitamin C. Vitamin C is essential for collagen synthesis in the body, and taking them together before exercise maximizes collagen incorporation into tendons and ligaments during physical activity. Alternatively, you can take it on an empty stomach in the morning or before bed. The important thing is to take it on a relatively empty stomach to improve absorption.

Does collagen help prevent injuries in runners?

Yes, hydrolyzed collagen supplementation has been shown in clinical studies to reduce joint pain in athletes and improve the functionality of tendons and ligaments. Collagen represents 90% of the composition of tendons and ligaments, and 60% of articular cartilage. With high-impact running, these structures progressively degrade. Supplementing with 10-15g daily of hydrolyzed collagen provides the specific amino acids (proline, glycine, hydroxyproline) your body needs to repair and strengthen these tissues.

Can you combine collagen with other running supplements?

Yes, collagen is compatible with most sports supplements. The most recommended combination is collagen + vitamin C, since vitamin C is essential for collagen synthesis. It also combines well with magnesium, omega-3, creatine and whey protein. The only precaution is not to take collagen at the same time as whey protein, because they compete for absorption. Leave at least 1-2 hours between both to maximize the assimilation of each one.
