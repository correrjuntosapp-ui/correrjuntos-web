Chicago's World-Class Lakefront Running

Chicago's crown jewel is the 18-mile Lakefront Trail, offering uninterrupted running along Lake Michigan with stunning skyline views. From sunrise runs at North Avenue Beach to sunset miles past Museum Campus, the lakefront defines Chicago's running culture. CorrerJuntos connects you with the Windy City's dedicated, all-weather running community.

Whether you're training for the Chicago Marathon in October, exploring Lincoln Park's tree-lined paths, or discovering the elevated 606 Trail through neighborhoods, Chicago offers world-class urban running. Find your crew and experience why Chicagoans run year-round, even through brutal winters.

Iconic Lakefront Trail

Run the legendary 18-mile paved path along Lake Michigan, from Edgewater to Hyde Park, with unobstructed skyline views and endless distance options.

Urban Park Networks

Explore Lincoln Park's extensive paths, Grant Park's downtown loops, or Humboldt Park's lagoon routes throughout Chicago's excellent park system.

The 606 Trail

Discover this elevated 2.7-mile former rail line connecting Wicker Park, Bucktown, Humboldt Park, and Logan Square through trendy neighborhoods.

Popular Running Areas in Chicago

  • Lakefront Trail - 18 miles of paved lakefront running, Chicago's signature route
  • Lincoln Park - Extensive paths through Chicago's largest park
  • Grant Park - Downtown loops with skyline and lake views
  • 606 Trail - 2.7-mile elevated greenway through hip neighborhoods

Major Running Events

The Chicago Marathon in October is one of the World Marathon Majors, famous for flat, fast courses and massive neighborhood support with 1.7 million spectators. Multiple parkrun locations, the Chicago Area Runners Association (CARA), and clubs like Fleet Feet Chicago organize year-round training groups. Chicago runners embrace all seasons, from summer lakefront runs to winter treadmill alternatives.
