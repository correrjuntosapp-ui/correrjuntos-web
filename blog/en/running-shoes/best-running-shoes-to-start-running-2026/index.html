The 10 best running shoes to start running in 2026

A beginner-focused comparison with comfortable, stable, and protective models to build consistency from day one.

Running Shoes · Mar 10, 2026 · 10-12 min read

When you are new to running, shoe choice has a huge impact on comfort and confidence. A good pair helps reduce injury risk, improves stability, and makes adaptation to impact much easier.

Beginner runners should prioritize balanced cushioning, dependable support, and an easy transition through each stride. These 10 models are solid picks for starting on road surfaces and mild urban routes.

How to choose running shoes as a beginner

Start with cushioning: enough to absorb impact, but not so soft that you lose control. Comfort should help you run regularly without excessive fatigue.

Stability and gait type matter too. If you are unsure, begin with a stable neutral shoe and adjust later if recurring discomfort appears.

For weight and drop, beginners usually do well with medium-weight shoes and a drop between 6 and 12 mm. That range supports gradual adaptation.

Also separate road vs trail running. Road shoes are best for asphalt and urban paths, while trail shoes are better for dirt, rocks, and uneven terrain.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need specific shoes to start running?

Yes. They provide impact protection and support designed for repetitive running motion.

How long should running shoes last?

Usually 600 to 800 km, depending on your weight, running form, surface, and weekly volume.

What shoes do beginner runners use?

Most beginners use daily trainers with balanced cushioning, such as Pegasus, Ghost, Nimbus, and Clifton.

What is the best shoe to start running?

The best one is the model that fits your foot shape and keeps you comfortable and stable during regular training.
