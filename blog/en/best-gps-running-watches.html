How to choose a GPS running watch

  • Multi-band GPS (L1+L5): reduces positioning error to under 3 m in cities. Without it, drift can reach 15-20 m under buildings or trees. All models in this list have it.
  • GPS battery: minimum 15 hours for most runners. For ultra-trail, look for 30+. Multi-band GPS uses 30-40% more battery than standard.
  • Optical heart rate: accuracy varies. Garmin Elevate 5 and COROS are the most reliable on the wrist. For max accuracy, use a chest strap.
  • Training metrics: VO2 max, training load, recovery status and daily suggestions are the most useful.
  • Weight: under 50 g is ideal. Above 60 g you notice it on long runs.
  • Display: AMOLED is vivid but drains battery. MIP is perfect in direct sunlight and uses less power.
Common mistake: buying by advertised battery life without checking GPS mode specs. Many watches claim "up to 20 hours" — that is standard GPS. With multi-band, battery can drop 30-40%. Always check the multi-band GPS battery figure.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best GPS running watch in 2026?

The Garmin Forerunner 265 for most runners: AMOLED, 24h GPS, advanced metrics and offline music for ~€420. On a budget, the COROS PACE 3 (~€230) delivers 80% for half the price.

Garmin or COROS for running?

Garmin has the most complete ecosystem. COROS delivers better value: more battery, less weight, lower price. For value: COROS PACE 3. For the full package: Garmin FR 265.

What is the difference between standard GPS and multi-band GPS?

Multi-band GPS (L1+L5) reduces positioning error to under 3 m in cities. Standard GPS can drift 10-20 m. All watches here have multi-band GPS.

Is the Apple Watch Ultra 2 good for serious running?

Yes, but GPS battery (~17h) falls short of Garmin/COROS. Ideal if you already have an iPhone and want one watch for everything. If running is the priority, Garmin FR 265 delivers more for €430 less.

How much should I spend on a GPS running watch?

Multi-band GPS + optical HR + solid metrics starts at ~€230 with the COROS PACE 3. Most runners will be well served at €250-420.

AMOLED or MIP — which is better for running?

AMOLED is more vibrant indoors but uses ~30% more battery. MIP is perfect in direct sunlight and lighter on battery. For outdoor running in bright conditions, MIP is functionally superior.

Is the COROS PACE 3 good for marathon running?

Excellent. With 38 hours of multi-band GPS, you will never run out of battery. Running Power, VO2 max, form analysis and free adaptive plans in COROS Training Hub. Hard to beat at €230 for long-distance.

Which GPS watch has the best battery for trail running?

Mid-distance trail: COROS PACE 3 (38h) and Polar Pacer Pro (35h) are best in their price range. Extreme ultra-trail: Amazfit T-Rex Ultra (50h multi-band GPS).
