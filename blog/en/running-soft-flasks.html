Comparison of the best soft flasks for running: Salomon, DORNNA, MARLOAR and TRIWONDER. Guide on collapsible bottles with capacities, prices and Amazon links.

Soft flasks (collapsible bottles) have revolutionized running hydration. They compress as you drink eliminating sloshing, weigh half as much as rigid bottles, fit in front pockets of any trail vest, and when empty take up the space of an energy bar.

But not all soft flasks are equal: TPU quality, valve design, mouth opening, and durability vary significantly between brands. We’ve analyzed over 12 models and selected the 8 best soft flasks in 2026, from premium Salomon to 2-packs for under €10.

Quick summary: Best 500 ml → Salomon 500ml. Best 250 ml → Salomon 250ml. Best budget pack → DORNNA 2x500ml. For gels → Salomon 150ml.

How to choose soft flasks for running

Choosing the right soft flask depends on your race type and vest. Key criteria:

  • Capacity: 500 ml is the standard for hydration. 250 ml for short races with frequent aid stations. 150 ml for homemade gels or energy mixes.
  • Mouth: 42 mm (wide mouth) for quick refilling at aid stations. 28 mm (narrow mouth) for races where you don’t refill or for gels.
  • Valve: high-flow valves (Salomon) allow fast drinking without hard suction. Bite valves prevent dripping but require more suction.
  • Material: food-grade BPA-free TPU. Cheap ones may taste like plastic the first few times.
  • Compatibility: verify the diameter fits your vest pockets. Most vests accept the standard Salomon format.
Tip: always carry two soft flasks: one with water and one with electrolyte drink or salts. This way you can alternate based on what you need at each point in the race.
