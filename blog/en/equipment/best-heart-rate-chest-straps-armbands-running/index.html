How to choose a running heart-rate strap

Chest vs arm accuracy: chest straps are usually more precise in rapid intensity changes. Arm sensors are often more comfortable and easier for daily use.

App compatibility: check Bluetooth and/or ANT+ support if you want pairing with watches, trainers, and multiple apps (Garmin, Strava, Zwift, etc.).

Comfort: if chest pressure bothers you, an arm monitor can improve consistency and user comfort.

Battery: evaluate battery life and charging or battery-replacement convenience based on training frequency.

Frequently asked questions

Is a chest strap better than an arm monitor?

It depends on your priority. Chest strap for maximum accuracy, arm monitor for comfort and convenience.

Are heart-rate straps more accurate than a watch?

Usually yes, especially in intervals and changing-intensity workouts.

Can I use it with Strava or Garmin?

Yes, if the model supports Bluetooth and/or ANT+ for your watch or app setup.
