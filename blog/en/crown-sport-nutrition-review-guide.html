I have been searching for a Spanish sports nutrition brand that takes carbohydrate science seriously for long-duration endurance sport. Most gels on the market pack 22–27g of carbohydrates per packet — fine for a 10K, not enough when you are running or pedalling for three hours plus. Crown Sport Nutrition goes against the grain: their Hyper line starts at 45g of carbohydrates per serving, with a maltodextrin:fructose ratio (1:0.8) designed to maximise intestinal absorption.

Crown products carry Informed Sport certification (anti-doping tested), are vegan-friendly across most of the range, and are formulated without artificial colours or sweeteners. The team behind the brand collaborates with university sports science researchers, which shows in the technical documentation behind each product. Frequently asked questions

Is Crown Sport Nutrition a reliable brand?

Yes. Crown Sport Nutrition is a Spanish brand founded by sports scientists that collaborates with university researchers. Key products carry Informed Sport certification (anti-doping tested) and are formulated without artificial colours or sweeteners. It has become a reference brand in triathlon, cycling and ultra-trail in Spain.

What is the difference between HyperGel 45 and HyperDrink 90?

HyperGel 45 is a 75g packet with 45g of carbohydrates, easy to carry in a jersey pocket or trail vest. HyperDrink 90 is a drink delivering 90g of carbohydrates in 500ml of water, designed for a cycling water bottle or turbo trainer. The gel has higher caloric density; the drink combines energy and hydration in one container.

Does HyperGel 45 need water?

Crown recommends drinking water alongside HyperGel 45 to aid absorption, unlike isotonic gels such as SiS GO. The 165mg of sodium per packet supports fluid retention but does not replace the need to drink. Take it at a water station during a race.
