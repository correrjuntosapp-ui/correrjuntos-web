Running Groups in Zaragoza 2026: Clubs, Meetups and Routes

Everything you need to know to find your running group in the Aragonese capital: clubs, free meetups, routes along the Ebro and tips for running in the cierzo wind.

Zaragoza has something that hooks runners. Perhaps it is the vastness of its riverbanks along the Ebro, that immense river that splits the city in two and offers kilometers of flat, open paths. Or perhaps it is the Aragonese character, direct and unpretentious, which translates into a close-knit, committed running community with no airs. If you are looking for running groups in Zaragoza, you are about to discover one of the most active and fastest-growing running scenes in Spain (World Athletics).

Whether you have just bought your first running shoes or you have been pounding the pavement for years, Zaragoza has options for everyone. From historic clubs with decades of tradition to informal groups that meet via WhatsApp for a long run along the Canal Imperial. In this guide we cover everything you need to find people to run with in Zaragoza and become part of this ever-growing community (ACSM).

Because running alone is fine, but running with company is something else. The benefits of group running are well documented: greater motivation, better training adherence, fewer injuries and, above all, much more fun. And in a city like Zaragoza, where the cierzo wind sometimes tests your resolve, having companions by your side makes the difference between staying on the sofa and heading out to tackle the kilometers (WHO).

Why Zaragoza is a great city for running

Zaragoza is Spain's fifth-largest city, yet it maintains a human scale that makes it perfect for runners. It lacks the overwhelming traffic of Madrid or the crowds of Barcelona, yet it offers top-tier sports infrastructure and generous green spaces for outdoor training.

The terrain works in the runner's favor: Zaragoza sits in the Ebro Valley, which means most of the city is flat as a board. The Ebro riverbanks, the Canal Imperial de Aragón paths and the urban parks offer kilometer after kilometer of flat routes, ideal both for beginners and for those looking for speed workouts on fast terrain. And if you want elevation, the hills on the outskirts (like the Galacho de Juslibol or the muelas of the Plana) are just a stone's throw away.

Zaragoza's continental climate has its nuances. Winters are cold and dry, summers are hot (though less extreme than in the south), and spring and autumn are simply perfect for running. But there is one factor that every Zaragoza runner knows and respects: the cierzo. This northwest wind, intense and persistent, is the great protagonist of the local climate. It can be your worst enemy or your best coach, depending on how you face it.

Most importantly, there are the people. Zaragozans are direct, loyal and generous. When you join a running group here, they welcome you without ceremony and without filters. Within a few sessions you are already one of the crew. That Aragonese authenticity shows in every meetup, every long run, every post-run beer through El Tubo.

The best routes for group running in Zaragoza

Zaragoza has routes for all tastes and levels. These are the most popular areas among Zaragoza's running groups:

Ebro Riverbank

The Ebro is the backbone of running in Zaragoza. The paths along both banks of the river offer over 10 kilometers of flat, wide, well-paved terrain. The classic route runs from the Puente de Piedra to the Puente del Tercer Milenio along one bank and back along the other, with breathtaking views of the Basílica del Pilar. It is the usual meeting point for most groups, especially at sunset.

If you extend eastward, you can reach Parque del Agua Luis Buñuel, a huge space with dirt paths and landscaped areas that adds variety to the route. Heading west, the riverbank continues to the Ranillas meander with quieter stretches and nature on both sides.

Parque Grande José Antonio Labordeta

Zaragoza's most beloved green lung is a classic for runners. Its main circuit is around 3 kilometers in perimeter, with wide paths, abundant shade from pine and plane trees, and a gentle slope that adds some muscular work without being demanding. Many Zaragoza clubs use Parque Grande as their base for interval and speed sessions.

The Cabañal area, in the upper part of the park, offers more technical dirt trails that appeal to urban trail enthusiasts. Plus, the connection with the Botanical Garden and the Canal Imperial makes it easy to extend runs to 8-10 km without leaving green space.

Canal Imperial de Aragón

A gem for those who want straight-line kilometers, car-free and with shade. The path alongside the Canal Imperial is completely flat, tree-lined for much of its length and extends for kilometers and kilometers south of the city. It is the favorite for weekend long runs: you can do 15, 20 or 25 km out and back without repeating a single meter.

Additionally, being partially sheltered by the tree canopy, on cierzo days the Canal Imperial offers some protection from the wind, making it the go-to refuge for runners when the wind blows hard on the Ebro riverbanks.

Galacho de Juslibol

For trail and nature lovers, the Galacho de Juslibol is a protected natural area just 7 kilometers from Zaragoza's city center. Ancient meanders of the Ebro have created a landscape of lagoons, cliffs and riparian vegetation that seems otherworldly. The trails are dirt, with some elevation and varied terrain, perfect for trail workouts without leaving the city.

Several Zaragoza trail running groups organize regular outings to the Galacho, especially on weekend mornings. It is a different kind of workout that breaks the monotony of asphalt and connects you with the nature surrounding the Aragonese capital.

Running and athletics clubs in Zaragoza

Zaragoza has a solid athletic tradition. The city is home to clubs of different profiles, from federated clubs with decades of history to more modern groups focused on social running. If you want to join a running group, these are the most notable options:

Zaragoza Runners

One of the most active and visible groups in the city. Zaragoza Runners was born as a social running community and has grown into a local reference. They organize weekly meetups with sub-groups by pace (from 6:30/km to 4:30/km), allowing runners of all levels to feel comfortable. Their philosophy prioritizes enjoyment over personal bests, though many of their members have achieved their best times training together. They usually meet on the Ebro Riverbank and at Parque Grande.

Club Atletismo Zaragoza

The quintessential historic club of the Aragonese capital. Founded decades ago, CA Zaragoza has sections for track athletics, middle distance, long distance and trail. They have RFEA-certified coaches, personalized training plans and their own competition calendar. It is the ideal club if you are looking for structure, technical guidance and a competitive environment that pushes you to improve. The membership fee includes a federation license and access to training facilities.

Simply Runners Zgz

A more informal group but incredibly active. Simply Runners started as a group of friends who wanted to run without the rigidity of a traditional club and has grown through word of mouth and social media. They meet two or three times a week at different points around the city, changing routes each time to keep things fresh. The atmosphere is relaxed and festive: the post-run beers are nearly as important as the workout itself. Ideal if you are looking for company without formal commitment.

Ibercaja Running Club

Backed by the Aragonese banking institution, Ibercaja Running Club offers free training sessions open to the entire community. They have professional coaches who lead the sessions and prepare plans for popular races like the Zaragoza Marathon or the 10K Pilar. It is an excellent entry point for those who want to start running from scratch with structure and professional guidance without paying a club fee.

Other notable clubs and groups

  • Running Femenino Zaragoza: An exclusively women's group that organizes meetups in a safe, motivating environment. They have grown significantly in recent years and are very active on social media.
  • Trail Aragón Running: Focused on mountain and trail races. They organize outings to the outskirts of Zaragoza and the Pre-Pyrenees, perfect for those seeking more than asphalt.
  • Club Atletismo La Salle: Based in the Actur neighborhood, they combine track athletics with popular running. A good option for families and young runners.
  • Nike Run Club Zaragoza: Nike periodically organizes free sessions led by pacers at various points around the city. Check their app for updated dates.
Local tip: In Zaragoza, most clubs let you try one or two sessions for free before asking for any commitment. Take advantage of this: try several groups before deciding. Each one has its own personality, and finding the right fit is the difference between running out of obligation and running with excitement every day.

Free meetups and social running

Not all running in Zaragoza happens through clubs. In fact, some of the most dynamic communities are informal groups organized through social media and apps like CorrerJuntos. These meetups are free, no-commitment and open to any level.

On Instagram, searching hashtags like #RunningZaragoza, #CorrerEnZaragoza or #ZaragozaRunners will turn up dozens of group profiles posting their weekly meetups. Many emerged during and after the pandemic, when runners who had always trained alone discovered that doing it with company completely transformed the experience.

Local running stores have also joined the trend of organizing free group runs. It is a practice that works for everyone: the store builds community and visibility, and runners get a guided meetup where they can also test gear. Stores like Forum Sport, Decathlon and specialist shops in the center organize open runs regularly.

Parkrun in Zaragoza

If you are not yet familiar with parkrun, it is a free, timed event held every Saturday at 9:00 AM in parks worldwide. It is always 5 km, you set your own pace, and everyone is welcome: from the 18-minute finisher to the person walking the 5 km enjoying the scenery.

As of 2026, Zaragoza does not yet have an official established parkrun, although the local running community has been requesting one for years and there are ongoing conversations to establish one at Parque del Agua Luis Buñuel. If parkrun Zaragoza launches, it will be an incredible meeting point for the city's running community, given the park's size and ideal conditions.

In the meantime, several local groups organize their own Saturday morning meetups with a similar format: 5 km, free, no pressure, and breakfast afterwards. It is the parkrun spirit applied to Zaragoza's reality. And if you want to participate in an official parkrun, the nearest ones are in Seville's Parque del Alamillo or at other events in Spain that you can check on the parkrun website.

If you want to stay informed about when parkrun launches in Zaragoza, follow them on social media. It will be a turning point for social running in the Aragonese capital.

Zaragoza's climate and the cierzo: how to adapt

Let us talk about the great protagonist of running in Zaragoza: the cierzo. This northwest wind is the city's defining climatic feature. It can blow in gusts exceeding 80 km/h, dramatically reduces the wind chill in winter, and dries out your skin and mucous membranes within minutes. But it also clears Zaragoza's skies to a flawless blue, and if you learn to live with it, it becomes the best mental resilience training you can imagine.

Zaragoza runners have their strategies for dealing with the cierzo:

  • Choose sheltered routes: The Canal Imperial de Aragón and Parque Grande, with their dense tree cover, offer good protection from the wind. The Ebro Riverbank, by contrast, is very exposed.
  • Start into the wind: A golden rule among locals: head out running into the cierzo and return with it at your back. This way the second half of your workout feels much more manageable.
  • Always carry a windbreaker: A lightweight windbreaker jacket is essential in Zaragoza from October to April. It makes the difference between a miserable workout and an enjoyable one.
  • Protect your airways: The cold, dry cierzo can irritate your throat and bronchial tubes. A buff or neck gaiter will help warm the air before you breathe it in.
  • Adjust the intensity: On strong cierzo days, lower the effort by a notch. Your usual pace into the wind is not your real pace. Do not get frustrated: you are training harder than the numbers suggest.

Beyond the cierzo, Zaragoza's continental climate has its advantages. Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) offer idyllic running conditions, with temperatures between 10 and 22 degrees and clear skies. Summers are hot (35-38°C highs) but dry, making them more bearable than in humid cities. In summer, groups move their meetups to 7:00-7:30 AM or push them to 20:30-21:00.

Winters are cold (lows near 0°C), but overnight frosts give way to sunny, dry days perfect for running well wrapped up. Rainfall is scarce in Zaragoza (barely 300 mm annually), so you will rarely have to cancel a workout due to bad weather.

Survival tip: On strong cierzo days, many Zaragoza groups change their meetup location to more sheltered routes or simply opt for an indoor track session. Being in a group gives you that flexibility: someone always knows an alternative when the wind blows too hard.

Popular races in Zaragoza

Zaragoza's race calendar is varied and growing. Practically every month there is an event that attracts runners from all over Aragón and beyond. Racing with your running group multiplies the experience.

The must-do events include:

  • CaixaBank Zaragoza Marathon (autumn): The big date on the calendar. An urban course passing by the city's main monuments, with an incredible atmosphere on the streets. It includes a marathon, half marathon and 10K, so there is a distance for everyone.
  • 10K Pilar (October): Coinciding with the Fiestas del Pilar, this night race brings together thousands of runners in a uniquely festive atmosphere. The combination of running and local festivities is irresistible.
  • Zaragoza City Half Marathon: A flat and fast course, ideal for chasing a personal best. Many runners choose it as a test before preparing for a full marathon.
  • San Silvestre Zaragoza (December): The race that closes the year. Thousands of participants, many in costume, fill the city center streets in a running festival that sends the year off in style.
  • Charity and neighborhood races: Throughout the year, various neighborhoods and associations organize accessible, affordable popular races. They are perfect for your first competitive experience or for running for a good cause.

Training with a group for a race creates a special bond. The shared nerves at the start line, the encouragement during the tough kilometers and the joint celebration at the finish are moments that turn training partners into true friends.

Frequently asked questions

What are the best running clubs in Zaragoza?

The most notable clubs include Zaragoza Runners (social running for all levels), Club Atletismo Zaragoza (historic and federated), Simply Runners Zgz (informal group with weekly meetups) and Ibercaja Running Club (free coached sessions with professional trainers). There are also specific groups like Running Femenino Zaragoza and Trail Aragón Running.

When do running groups in Zaragoza meet?

Most meet on weekday afternoons (18:30-20:00 in winter, 19:30-21:00 in summer) and weekend mornings (8:00-10:00). In summer many move to 7:00 AM to avoid the heat, and on strong cierzo days they switch to sheltered routes or more favorable schedules.

Can beginners join running groups in Zaragoza?

Absolutely yes. Groups like Zaragoza Runners and Simply Runners Zgz are very welcoming to beginners and have sub-groups by pace. Ibercaja Running Club offers coached training for all levels. On CorrerJuntos you can filter by beginner level to find groups where nobody will judge you for your pace.

What is the best time of year to run in Zaragoza?

Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) are the best seasons, with temperatures between 10 and 22 degrees and clear skies. Winters are cold but dry and sunny (perfect with proper gear), and summers are manageable by running early. The key factor is the cierzo: check the wind forecast before choosing your route.

What is the cierzo and how does it affect running in Zaragoza?

The cierzo is a strong northwest wind characteristic of the Ebro Valley that can exceed 80 km/h. It reduces the wind chill, makes running difficult and dries out the skin. Local runners choose sheltered routes (Canal Imperial, Parque Grande), start into the wind to return with it at their back, wear windbreakers and protect their airways with a buff.

What are the best running routes in Zaragoza?

The most popular routes are the Ebro Riverbank (both banks, 10+ flat km with views of the Basilica del Pilar), Parque Grande José Antonio Labordeta (3 km loop with shade), Canal Imperial de Aragón (flat, tree-lined, ideal for long runs) and Galacho de Juslibol (nature and trail paths 7 km from the center).

Are there free running groups in Zaragoza?

Yes, there are many free options. Ibercaja Running Club offers free open training sessions. Simply Runners Zgz operates without fees. Local running stores organize open runs. And on CorrerJuntos you can join meetups created by other Zaragoza runners completely free. Many clubs also offer free trial sessions.
